8 REFORMS DEMANDS THAT WOULD DECREASE POLICE VIOLENCE
8cantwait calls for cities to adopt 8 common sense policing policies, like banning chokeholds, strangleholds and shooting at moving vehicles and requiring police officers to issue a warning before shooting and make comprehensive reports on every use of force.

But none of these demands strike at the fundamental problem: that police forces continue to exist and terrorize our neighborhoods. A lot of their reforms have already been implemented across major cities in the United States, yet police violence continues unabated.

Below is a list of 8 demands that are centered around abolition.








EXPEL POLICE UNIONS
THE AFL-CIO NEEDS TO EXPEL & BREAK POLICE UNIONS
This all has to start with the AFL-CIO expelling police unions and then ultimately breaking them. No union contract/no collective bargaining. We saw how effective that was with Scott Walker & the teachers unions in 2010 and we honestly can't be coy about using the same tactics. Everything that follows will be undermined in 1-5 years if that's not step one.

Community oversight boards don’t work while you’ve got the existing police unions. You can see this with the way the Rochester, NY Police Union undermined the Police Accountability Board to make it utterly toothless - it can make recommendations but it can't actually hold police accountable.

DEFUND THE POLICE
JOBS & EDUCATION NOT JAILS & INCARCERATION
Cities all across America are spending way too much on their police forces. 41.2% of Oakland's general fund expenditures are police spending. Meanwhile half of Oakland students don't have access to computers thanks to billionaires and charter school interests, homelessness is surging, and lead poisoning is worse than Flint but the city has to has had to rely on federal funds to make changes.. Oakland is just one in a sea of US cities that have been undergoing this phenomenon: our taxes have increasingly gone towards criminalization, policing, and mass incarceration at the expense of basic infrastructure and safety nets. St. Louis spends 31.8% of their budget on police funding. Atlanta: 29.7%. Houston: 35.0%. Detroit: 30.0%. Baltimore: 25.6%. Orlando: 32.3%.

Also, we should also be paying police brutality settlements out of police pension funds instead of taxpayer funds.

SURVEILLANCE, GAS, HOLOGRAPHIC SIGHT-EQUIPPED M4S, TANKS, DRONES...

HIRING FREEZE
A GRADUAL PATH TOWARDS NO POLICE
It's pretty simple, institute a nationwide police department hiring freeze. As each cop retires, their salary gets transferred to a community safety/welfare program. Cities should reduce overtime and other perks to encourage cops to retire.

RELEASE PRISONERS
40,000 PRISONERS IN THE US ARE IN JAIL ON CANNABIS CHARGES THAT ARE NO LONGER EVEN ILLEGAL
Police interaction is often peoples’ entry point into the prison industrial complex and the corrupt criminal justice system. Cops routinely plant evidence, commit perjury, and extract false confessions under duress. Even if we set aside the civil rights violations of each of these, it then puts people in the prison pipeline, which is difficult to escape. The US has more prisoners than China, and that’s in raw numbers. With a third of China’s population the US imprisons a higher number of people, and is about 7:1 per capita. Part of the reason is that the rise of private detention and prison-for-profit has provided a perverse incentive to fill up those prisons. A quick way to break that pipeline is to cut it off at the source, that initial contact with cops.

IMPLEMENT ALTERNATIVES
LET’S ENACT AND FUND COMMUNITY SAFETY PROGRAMS TO ENSURE OUR SAFETY
Public safety is a complex and multifaceted social issue. Why would we expect police to be able to take care of those experiencing homelessness, deal with the psychological aspects of addiction, or de-escalate tense situations? In fact, it makes you wonder what exactly the police are good at doing. We have a wealth of professionals who can deal with various facets of these social issues, and they don’t carry guns. Almost every kind of restorative justice, alternative dispute resolution, and community oversight offers superior outcomes to policing.

ABOLISH POLICE
NO COPS, LESS CRIMES
To “fight crime” we don’t need more military-grade cops, we need to put resources into programs and systems that address our basic needs. There’s less crime when there’s more jobs, more educational opportunities, more arts programs, more community centers, more mental health resources. Police are ineffective at solving crimes, and in most communities, they’re actually making things less safe. Not just through police brutality, although there’s plenty of that, it’s the whole criminal justice system, including the prison industrial complex, the drug war, and immigration enforcement that is the root cause of ongoing violence in communities across America.
